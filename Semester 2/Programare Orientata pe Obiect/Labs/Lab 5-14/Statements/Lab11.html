Details for Hybris

Solvable To Lab Number
14

It has been drizzling with unremitting patters for the past few days and nights. Outside, the main courtyard leading into the gardens appears to have altered its basic geometry into something resembling a marsh, made humid and dark in the shadow of the Spire. The enveloping darkness of yet another overcast day makes the already somber lighting of the Ancient City fade into a suffocating blanket of shade. Huric and another dozen or so archmages are standing before you and your fellow Spire initiates, everyone drenched in rainfall and firmly planted to their shins in the muck. Jutting out of the pond, rising behind your superiors with an ominous slant that suggests archaic patterns of architecture lies a runic altar the likes of which you have seldom seen described or sketched in the odd crumbling tome.

Huric had explained to you what the object was – the offspring of an unlikely alchemical wedlock of sorts, an experiment that would combine the disused potency of the Ancient City's underground leys and the cutting-edge arcane refinement of the capital's highest ranking minds. A runic altar the likes of which the known world has never seen. You would have to attempt and weave your most complex enchantment into its infrastructure, to test its capabilities. In theory, once affixed, the enchantment could be manipulated by untrained users as well as astute scholars of the arcane – whether it would be for the better or for the worse, or whether it would even work at all – would remain to be seen.

Create a graphical user interface (GUI) for the problem you have been working on (Labs 5,6,7,8), using Qt. You must implement the design of the interface and the list/table displaying the repository entities (administrator mode) should be populated. The GUI must be created and coded manually (no Qt Designer!). You can see an example at: GUI examples

Note: While there will be no automated tests for this or the next assignment upload here is mandatory, please upload when you are done and not just during your lab.

Bonus (0.2 p)

Create a graphic representation of the data in your application. You have an example at the link above: a bar chart representing the number of songs for each artist. Your representations can be a bar chart, a pie chart or another type of chart. You can even use circles or rectangles or any other geometric shapes to “draw” your data. Deadline lab 12.

Hint: You can use QPainter, QGraphicsScene or a special widget designed for plotting and data visualisation – QCustomPlot.
