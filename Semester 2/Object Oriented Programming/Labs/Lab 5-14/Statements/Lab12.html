Details for Walled-in

Solvable To Lab Number
14

Invisible clouds can only be imagined as the roars of thunderclaps wage war somewhere in the heights beyond the overhanging moss. The unnatural surges that singe the very air around you make your gut swell with apprehension. Monolithic and stationary, the altar before you has begun to float, activated for the first time in centuries by forces it had never before absorbed. Huric and the other archmages are overseeing the convergent weaving, still dispersed in a half-moon around the courtyard. Grim determination appears carved in most of the elders' miens, while the novices closest to the altar have their faces smitten with the waxy hue of terror. In mere hours your enchantments would be collectively affixed to the archaic construct. It would all be over. As you purposefully distract yourself from the biting cold climbing up your submerged feet, you wonder whether this experiment will draw any unforeseen costs – and if it shall, what would be their magnitude?

The turrets of the Ancient City had been powered to the pinnacle of their capabilities – and yet wild wrendigger attacks had only increased. What were the archmages hoping to achieve through this, and what of the Guard captains? Pushed beyond their limits, the turrets lining the outer walls could very well change their guise from benevolent guardians to crippling hazards – despite Huric and the others' placid guarantees of safety. In the midst of the downpour, contemplating the scope of your combined channeling, you find ever greater difficulty in imagining how this endeavor would harbor any victorious outcome.
